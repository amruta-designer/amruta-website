Role Product Designer (UI · UX)

Company Bupa

Problem Space

The Problem to Solve

Current pages failed to communicate value, guide users toward booking, or build the confidence needed for healthcare purchasing decisions.

  • Low Conversion & Unclear Journey Flow

    Pages failed to communicate value or guide users toward booking, resulting in below-par conversion rates and engagement metrics.

  • Poor Usability & Fragmented Page Design

    Disconnected pages with unclear navigation created friction in information discovery and task completion across the experience.

  • Lack of User Trust & Confidence

    Insufficient service information and aftercare messaging undermined credibility in a context where trust is essential to healthcare purchasing.

  • No Cohesive, User-Validated Experience

    Absence of journey mapping and consistent patterns caused funnel abandonment — each page felt disconnected from the next.

  • Fragmented Price Comparison

    Service details were scattered across dropdowns with booking links buried across disconnected pages, making comparison near-impossible.

  • Poor Scanability & Side-by-Side Comparison

    Users could not compare services side-by-side, requiring repeated scrolling and creating unnecessary cognitive load.

Approach

From Insight to Impact

A structured, research-led process that expanded from isolated page fixes to a unified end-to-end experience across the platform.

Phase 01

Discovery & Research

Conducted qualitative and quantitative research to surface user needs, pain points, and task flows. Analysed drop-off data, ran customer interviews, and mapped existing journeys to identify where trust and clarity broke down.

  • Identified high exit rates on service detail pages
  • Users cited unclear pricing and aftercare as primary blockers
  • Heatmaps revealed booking CTAs were being missed entirely
  • Regulatory and clinical requirements documented early
Phase 02

Collaborative Workshops

Facilitated workshops mapping user needs, task flows, and regulatory requirements across stakeholder groups. Journey mapping sessions brought clinical, marketing, and engineering perspectives into alignment early, preventing rework downstream.

  • Cross-functional journey mapping sessions
  • Design sprint to rapidly validate IA approaches
  • Stakeholder alignment on trust and clinical content requirements
Phase 03

Prototyping & Validation

Built interactive prototypes to test the redesigned information architecture and page patterns. User testing validated the new structure before any code was written, reducing costly iteration cycles in production.

  • Moderated usability testing with target patient cohorts
  • Iterated on price comparison table design across 3 rounds
  • A/B tested trust signal placements on landing page
  • Mobile-first prototypes tested on actual devices
Phase 04

Design & Delivery

Designed the full suite of 21 service pages using validated patterns, reusable components, and consistent design tokens. Collaborated closely with developers throughout delivery to ensure fidelity and maintain design intent across the responsive breakpoints.

  • Redesigned landing page + 21 PAYG service pages
  • Unified price comparison table replacing scattered dropdowns
  • Clinical evidence and aftercare integrated into page templates
  • Mobile-first delivery across all breakpoints

Reflections

Key Insights & Learnings

What the project reinforced about designing digital healthcare experiences at scale.

Empathy and Speed Aren't Opposing Forces

Clear, human tone of voice combined with fast task completion improved both satisfaction scores and conversion. Designing for emotional confidence and functional efficiency aren't competing goals — they reinforce each other.

Research-Backed Architecture Delivers ROI

User-validated information architecture and navigation reduced time-to-key-actions and exits. The upfront investment in workshops and testing paid back through fewer post-launch iterations and measurable commercial uplift.

Consistent Patterns Build Confidence

Recognisable layouts and consistent behaviours across 21 pages accelerated user movement through the funnel and increased journey trust. Once users understood one page, every subsequent page felt familiar — reducing decision fatigue.

Trust Drives Conversions

Addressing clinical evidence, aftercare content, and pricing transparency directly reduced user uncertainty and drove purchases. In healthcare, trust isn't a nice-to-have — it's a prerequisite to any commercial action.
